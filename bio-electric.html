The Practice of Kumbhaka and Rhythmic Control

Pranayama is the science of directing the body's bio-electric energy via the breath. By utilizing a "Box Breath" or "Square Breath" technique, you synchronize the heart rate with the nervous system. The practice requires a conscious inhalation, a retention (holding), an exhalation, and a final suspension—each performed to a steady count of 4 to 6 seconds.

This rhythmic holding, known as Kumbhaka, allows for a higher concentration of oxygen and CO2 exchange, stabilizing the electrical fluctuations of the mind and creating a "conductive" calm throughout the physical body.
